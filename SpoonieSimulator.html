This activity will take you through a day in the life of a spoonie, or someone who uses the Spoon Theory in their everyday life. You have a finite number of spoons, and a list of things to get done. Try to get as many of them done as you can, but don't feel bad if you can't! There is no way to lose or win, just different ways to play.
Spoonie Simulator
The spoon theory is a way to explain chronic illness to people who might not understand what it's like to have a chronic illness, using spoons as a way to show someone's energy level. Each person has a certain amount of energy in a day, and they can 'spend' a number of spoons to complete tasks and chores. When someone is out of spoons, they aren't able to do anything that takes energy until they have regained more spoons by doing something fun, eating, or resting. Every day activities take spoons, even things that you might not realize!
Welcome to Spoonie Simulator a 'choose your own adventure' style project where you play as someone with a chronic illness, navigating a simple and quiet day in their life. This was made by Riley Kunz for the DGIM 2521 final. 
A chronic illness is a kind of sickness that sticks around and you don't get better from like you would from a cold or the flu. Being chronically ill takes a lot of energy, which is why Christine Miserandino created something called the 'Spoon Theory'.
Things like making breakfast, taking a shower, and getting dressed are all things that can take spoons from someone who is chronically ill. Sometimes chronically ill people will have to wait several days to do something if it takes a lot of spoons. Most of the time, going to bed and waking up will completely replenish someone's supply of spoons, but sometimes a chronically ill person will wake up and have less than their average amount of spoons. On those days, it's usually difficult to get out of bed and do anything, because they don't have enough energy to get started.
